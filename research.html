Our Research Projects

Our laboratory focuses on innovative research in soil science and precision agriculture, combining traditional knowledge with cutting-edge technologies to solve real-world agricultural challenges.

Precision Soil Mapping

Our undergrad student Kusum Budha is working on GIS-based soil mapping to identify spatial variability in soil properties. This research helps optimize fertilizer application and land management practices in Palpa.

Learn More

Biochar and Microbis

This research project evaluates the microbial safety and biological functioning of urine-enriched biochar amended soils. It focuses on assessing key microbial indicators (E. coli and Salmonella), total microbial populations, and soil enzyme activities to understand potential health risks and soil quality responses.

2025-2026
Learn More

Nutrient Loaded Biochar

This research evaluates urea-enriched, cattle urine-enriched, and human urine-enriched biochar under reduced urea application to improve soil fertility, optimize nitrogen utilization, and promote environmentally friendly nutrient management strategies for maize-based systems.

2025-2026
Learn More

Biochar-Biofertilizer synergy

The research involves the inoculation of biochar with Azotobacter and phosphate-solubilizing bacteria (PSB) to enhance nutrient availability and biological activity in soil. It aims to assess how this integrated approach improves nitrogen utilization, soil health, and crop performance in chilli-based production systems.

2025-2026
Learn More

Mulch Matters

This research project evaluates the effects of different mulching materials on soil moisture conservation and nutrient retention in crop production systems. The study investigates plastic mulch, sawdust, straw, and sugarcane bagasse to understand their influence on soil microclimate and soil fertility.

2025-2026
Learn More

Crop Health Monitoring

This research project focuses on nitrogen monitoring and yield prediction using RGB drone imagery and vegetation indices-based models. It evaluates the relationship between canopy reflectance patterns and crop nitrogen status to develop low-cost, field-scale decision support tools for precision nitrogen management.

2025-2026
