About Our Lab

The Soil Science and Precision Agriculture Lab is a research laboratory led by Krishna Aryal (Assistant Professor) at Institute of Agriculture and Animal Science (IAAS), Tribhuvan University. This lab is dedicated to understanding the mysteries beneath our feet and translating that understanding into practical tools for improving agricultural productivity, soil health and environmental sustainability across the world.

This lab integrates soil science, Waste Management, remote sensing, geospatial technologies, data analytics and smart agricultural systems to support sustainable and climate resilient agriculture.

Prof. Aryal holds a M.Sc. in Soil Science and has published extensively in peer-reviewed journals. He is dedicated to training the next generation of agricultural researchers and collaborating with international institutions to advance soil science in Nepal.

Research Focus Areas

Soil Health and Fertility

Comprehensive analysis of soil nutrient profiles, microbial communities, organic matter dynamics, integrated nutrient management and precision nutrient management strategies for sustainable cropping systems.

Remote Sensing and GIS

UAV-based multispectral imaging, satellite data integration, digital soil mapping and spatial modelling for variability assessments at field scale, predictive analytics, crop suitability analysis, crop health monitoring and sustainable land use planning.

Agricultural Waste Recycling and Circular Agriculture

Utilization of agriculture, livestock, and household waste through composting, biochar production, nutrient recovery, and circular bioeconomy approaches.

Conservation Agriculture

Investigation of conservation agriculture practices including minimum tillage/zero tillage, mulching and cover cropping to improve soil health, moisture conservation, nutrient cycling, carbon sequestration, and long-term sustainability of agroecosystem.

Vision

Mission

To advance soil health science and deploy precision agriculture solutions that improve livelihoods and sustain natural resources.

Core Values

  • Scientific Rigour - Evidence-based research, accuracy, and methodological excellence
  • Farmer-First Focus - Research questions shaped by real challenges in the field, benefits of smallholder farmers in all innovations
  • Environmental Stewardship - Promoting sustainable practices that protect soil, water, and ecosystem health
  • Knowledge Sharing - Fostering open learning, collaboration, and dissemination of scientific knowledge for broader impact
